About Me

I build things. I teach things. Usually both at once.

Somewhere around my second year of university, I realised that the best engineers I admired weren't just technically sharp — they could explain what they built in a way that made it feel obvious. I wanted to be that kind of person.

That's probably why teaching has been such a constant part of my life. For five years, I've been tutoring students — quietly, without a platform or marketing, just word of mouth from people whose understanding actually improved. It's taught me more about how technology works, and how people think, than any course I've taken.

"The moment a student's face changes — when something that confused them for weeks suddenly makes sense — that's the feeling I try to recreate when I'm designing a system. I want users to feel that same clarity."

My technical work has grown out of genuine curiosity: what happens when AI actually helps someone, not just when it technically functions? My capstone project, an AI-powered gym assistant, started from a simple question: what would a coach that actually listens look like as software? The projects that followed — a learning platform, a workflow system — asked similar questions about real institutional friction I'd watched up close as a Teaching Assistant.

I have graduated this year and joined Chorate AI at Qatar Science and Technology Park. I'm not sure exactly where things will go from here, but I'm genuinely excited to find out. I'm building in a region that's investing seriously in its digital future, and I want to be part of that.

AI-Powered Fitness Technology · Recommendation Systems · User-Centered Design
This project started with a simple question: what would a personal trainer look like as software — one that actually adapts to you, remembers your progress, and gives you feedback that makes sense for where you are, not where someone else is? The answer turned into my most complete piece of engineering work to date.

I've spent years explaining things to people at different levels. The hardest part is always calibrating — knowing how much detail to give, when to simplify, when to push further. That's exactly the problem this system tries to solve for fitness. It's not about the reps; it's about whether the person keeps going. I designed the AI guidance layer with that in mind: not optimal in theory, but helpful in practice.

The Whole Person

Beyond the Work

The things that aren't on a CV but probably shape how I work more than anything on it.

🏏
Cricket
Cricket taught me patience before I knew I needed it. The game doesn't reward rushing. You read the situation, you make a call, and sometimes you wait for three hours for the right moment to act. I think about that when I'm debugging something that isn't making sense — sometimes the answer only comes if you slow down and look properly.
📖
Continuous Learning
I don't think I've ever finished a week without reading something I didn't have to read. Documentation, articles, course material — not because I'm disciplined about it, but because I genuinely get curious. The moment I stop finding something interesting is usually the moment I need to look at it from a different angle.
🤝
Relationships & Teamwork
The best work I've done has always involved other people — not just working alongside them, but actually thinking together. I've learned that disagreement, when it comes from people who respect each other, produces better outcomes than consensus from people who are being polite. I'd rather have someone push back on my idea than agree with it out of convenience.
🧭
Leadership by Showing Up
I've never been interested in leadership as a title. But I've learned that leadership is mostly about consistency — being someone people can rely on, following through on small things, and not waiting to be asked before you help. The students who kept coming back to my tutoring sessions weren't coming back for my expertise. They were coming back because I was there, every time.
🌍
Cultural Curiosity
Growing up in Doha and studying at a university with students from dozens of countries has made me genuinely curious about how differently people think. I've tutored students from backgrounds very different from mine, and every time I've had to find a new way to explain something, I've understood the thing better myself. Perspective is a technical advantage.
🏗️
The Long Game
I'm not in a rush. I want to build things that matter — not things that impress people at a glance and fall apart later. That takes time, and I'm okay with that. The tutoring practice I've run for five years wasn't built overnight. Neither will the career I'm working toward. I'd rather build something real slowly than something impressive quickly.
